About Us

OUR VISION

Innovate Ghana is a an annual competition of GREMKAY International’s, designed to encourage Ghanaian students to solve national and community d evelopment problems of today. By teaching concepts of Human Centered Design, STEM/STEAM and Entrepreneurship, the program seeks to achieve the following outcomes:

  1. Empower the youth to create innovative solution in cross-disciplinary teams
  2. Encourage young women to enter the STEM fields
  3. Connect local professionals in the STEM Fields with students and encourage mentoring
  4. Foster teamwork across disciplines as an example of real world collaboration

GREMKAY International’s mission is to educate, enlighten, and empower unemployed youth for productive and gainful employment in Ghana. We offer a number of training opportunities for youth through online courses and an annual design competition called I nnovate Ghana. Through this program, our ultimate goal is to develop a pipeline of skilled STEM talent to not only drive industrial development in Ghana, but to inspire a culture of innovation and empower the next generation of youth to develop local solutions for local problems.

Our Pillars

  1. IDEATE: Inspire innovation through competition This component supports the identification and development of innovative products and services through our annual competitions. Key activities include the High School Competition, the Tertiary Competition and Human Centered Design workshops across the country.
  2. CREATE: Develop products and solutions for national development (# concepts converted to businesses / taken to market). This component supports the development and incubation of product ideas that are developed from the competitions. Here, we focus on preparing these innovators for local incubators, potential investors by supporting them with technical assistance for research and development, prototyping and business model development.
  3. DEVELOP: Nurture and grow youth skills in engineering, design, manufacturing & entrepreneurship. This component supports the development of youth skills in STEM through in-person training, online courses and mentoring for students, particularly women in engineering.
